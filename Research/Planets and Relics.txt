A second structural point is crucial for phase mapping. Ordinary planets mostly affect phases through the core rules rather than via printed effect text: they are controlled during **Action Phase tactical actions**, exhausted/spent as **resources or influence**, readied during the **Status Phase**, and used for **Agenda Phase voting** if they have influence. Traits themselves have no inherent rules text, but they matter for **exploration** and objectives; tech specialties matter when researching technology. **Space stations** in ThunderŌĆÖs Edge are a special case: they have planet cards and can be spent/readied like planets, but they are **not planets** for objectives, elect-planet agendas, or home-system-control checks. ## Scope and phase-coding

The core rules baseline used throughout the tables is:

- **Setup**: home planet cards are distributed in **Step 5**, and starting technologies/units are gained in **Step 11**. Planet cards and legendary planet ability cards are placed near the board during setup; the relic deck is shuffled in **Step 8**. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn13view0Ņłéturn13view1Ņłéturn13view3Ņłü
- **Action Phase**: tactical actions follow the quick-reference sequence **Activation ŌåÆ Movement ŌåÆ Space Combat ŌåÆ Invasion ŌåÆ Production**. Gaining control of an uncontrolled, traited planet triggers **exploration** in Prophecy of Kings rules. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn15view0Ņłéturn12view4Ņłü
- **Status Phase**: the phase includes **Score Objectives**, **Reveal Public Objective**, **Ready Cards**, and other cleanup. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn14view2Ņłéturn15view0Ņłü
- **Agenda Phase**: after Mecatol Rex is opened, the phase runs **Reveal Agenda ŌåÆ Vote ŌåÆ Resolve Outcome** twice, then **Ready Planets**. Influence is used to vote. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn14view3Ņłéturn16view0Ņłü

To keep the report readable, the tables use short phase codes:

**Planet codes**

- **Setup**: card placed/gained in setup or via setup-side system rules
- **A/Tac**: standard tactical-action control/invasion relevance
- **A/Strat**: can be exhausted for resources or influence during strategic actions in the Action Phase
- **Exp**: explored on first control if eligible
- **Tech**: tech-specialty skip during research
- **A/Pass**: specific pass / end-of-turn / after-activate timing
- **Combat**: explicit combat-step hook
- **Status**: explicit status-phase hook, or standard readying / objective relevance
- **Agenda**: voting or explicit agenda hook
- **Obj**: notable objective or public-objective lock interaction
- **SS**: ThunderŌĆÖs Edge space-station special rules
- **Leg**: legendary-ability-card interaction

**Timing-type codes**

- **P** persistent
- **T** triggered
- **O** optional
- **M** mandatory
- **1x** one-time

## Rules baseline for planets and relics

Planets matter to the game in a few global ways that repeat across almost every row. A controlled planetŌĆÖs card stays in the playerŌĆÖs play area; readied planets may be spent for resources or influence, exhausted planets may not. Technology-specialty planets can be exhausted to ignore a matching prerequisite when researching a technology. Legendary planets grant a separate legendary-planet ability card when controlled. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn16view0Ņłéturn12view3Ņłéturn12view2Ņłü

For home systems specifically, losing control of one or more planets in your home system prevents you from scoring public objectives. Also, home planets and Mecatol Rex are not explored under the exploration rules. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn9view0Ņłéturn12view4Ņłü

Relics are gained primarily by purging relic fragments, and relic fragments come from planet/frontier exploration. Relics sit faceup in a playerŌĆÖs play area, their abilities can be used from there, and relics themselves cannot be traded under standard Prophecy of Kings rules. ThunderŌĆÖs Edge adds a notable exception by printing a relic that explicitly permits its own trading. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn16view1Ņłéturn12view4Ņłéturn11view0Ņłü

### Replacement and deprecation appendix
