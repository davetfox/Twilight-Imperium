# Twilight Imperium Fourth Edition Phase-Mapped Card Catalogue

## Executive summary

This report maps the **phase-relevant card ecosystem** of *Twilight Imperium Fourth Edition* across the **Base Game**, **Prophecy of Kings**, **Codex Volumes I–IV**, and **Thunder’s Edge**, with special attention to **replacement logic** and **Omega versions**. The core pattern is stable: the base game establishes the eight strategy cards, public/basic technologies, faction technologies, promissory notes, and the original action-card timing windows; *Prophecy of Kings* adds leaders, legendary planets, more technologies and promissory notes, and revises the **Diplomacy** and **Construction** strategy cards; the Codices introduce **Omega / replacement** content and new factions; and *Thunder’s Edge* further expands the card pool while also replacing some earlier materials again, including strategy-card and technology updates. citeturn7view0turn24search3turn19search0turn24search8turn24search0

Two practical conclusions matter most for rules work. First, **phase mapping is best done by timing window**, not by component category: most relevant cards hook into one of these windows—**strategy selection**, **strategic action**, **activation**, **movement**, **space cannon offense**, **start of combat round**, **after rolls**, **invasion substeps**, **production**, **status start/end**, and **agenda reveal/vote/outcome**. Second, **version control matters**: if you are using all current official material, you should treat **PoK Diplomacy/Construction**, **Codex Omega cards**, **Codex III leader Omegas**, and the **Thunder’s Edge updates** as the live versions where they replace older cards. citeturn20view0turn20view1turn21view0turn21view1turn19search1turn35view0turn24search0

For source quality, I prioritized **Fantasy Flight / official rules PDFs and product-document pages** for set identity, phase structure, and replacement statements. I used **Twilight Imperium Wiki** and, where helpful, **TI Rules** and **TI4 Kiosk** as secondary catalogues for card-level text summaries, Omega markings, and phase tagging, because the official sources do not provide one single parsed, machine-readable database for every card across all sets. Where a card’s latest text is only clearly exposed in secondary catalogues, I flag that explicitly. citeturn9view0turn24search3turn24search8turn19search0turn19search1turn12search0turn11search6

## Scope and source model

The official rules reference defines the four main phases—**Strategy, Action, Status, Agenda**—and the tactical-action substructure used during the Action Phase. In particular, the rules identify **movement**, **space cannon offense**, **invasion**, **bombardment**, **commit ground forces**, **space cannon defense**, **ground combat**, and **production** as distinct timing steps or related windows. Those are the anchors used in the phase tags throughout this report. citeturn20view0turn20view1turn20view4turn20view5turn21view0turn21view1

The base rules also establish that players begin with faction technologies and promissory notes, and that promissory notes themselves are timing-text components. *Prophecy of Kings* then adds **leaders**, **24 promissory notes**, **128 technology cards**, **40 planet cards**, and **4 legendary planet ability cards**, while also revising **Diplomacy** and **Construction** and instructing players to integrate the new components into the base game. citeturn9view0turn21view2turn4view0turn7view0turn7view1

For version control, the community-maintained **Version Tracking** and **Omegas** catalogues are especially useful because they summarize the official Omega convention: **Ω** marks an updated card, and the updated card **replaces** the earlier printing of the same name/type. Those secondary catalogues also track later **ΩΩ** styling where Thunder’s Edge or later Codices supersede earlier Omega revisions. I treat those pages as secondary but highly useful indexing tools, cross-checked against the official Codex and Thunder’s Edge document archive. citeturn19search1turn19search0turn24search3turn24search0

## Version and replacement framework

The table below is the practical replacement map for live rules work.

| Component family | Original live set | Later official replacement path | Current live guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diplomacy** strategy card | Base | PoK revised version; also indexed as **Diplomacy Ω** in later all-expansions catalogues | Use the **PoK/Codex-indexed Diplomacy Ω** version in full-expansion play |

| **Wormhole Generator** | Base | Codex I **Ω** | Use **Ω** |
| **Yin Spinner** | Base | Codex I **Ω** | Use **Ω** |
| **Magmus Reactor** | Base | Codex I **Ω** | Use **Ω** |
| **Stymie / War Funding / Cybernetic Enhancements / Acquiescence / Greyfire Mutagen** promissory notes | Base | Codex I **Ω** promissory replacements | Use **Ω** versions |
| **Naalu leaders** | PoK | Codex III **Ω** agent/commander adjustments | Use **Codex III Ω** versions |
| **Yin leaders** | PoK | Codex III **Ω** full leader pass | Use **Codex III Ω** versions |

These replacement relationships are directly supported by the official PoK integration instructions, the official FFG document archive listing all Codices and TE support files, the TE rulebook snippets that describe replacement of earlier tech copies, and the secondary strategy/Omega/faction-tech catalogues that track which version is current in all-expansion play. citeturn7view0turn24search3turn24search0turn19search0turn35view0turn31view2

The most important version caveat is that *Thunder’s Edge* is not just additive. The official rulebook snippet states that, if you have previous versions, you replace card sets including **eight copies each of Magen Defense Grid and X-89 Bacterial Weapon**, and the strategy-card catalogue shows **Construction ΩΩ** and **Warfare Ω** in full-expansion play. That means any database that mixes base, Codex I, Codex IV, and Thunder’s Edge tech texts without version tagging will produce false timing calls. citeturn24search0turn35view0turn35view1turn35view3

## Phase map used in this catalogue

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    SP[Strategy Phase]
    AP[Action Phase]
    ST[Status Phase]
    AG[Agenda Phase]

    SP --> AP
    AP --> TA[Tactical Action]
    AP --> SA[Strategic Action]
    AP --> CA[Component Action]
    AP --> PASS[Pass]

    TA --> ACT[Activation]
    ACT --> MOV[Movement]
    MOV --> SCO[Space Cannon Offense]
    SCO --> SC[Space Combat]
    SC --> INV[Invasion]
    INV --> BOMB[Bombardment]
    INV --> CGF[Commit Ground Forces]
    INV --> SCD[Space Cannon Defense]
    INV --> GC[Ground Combat]
    INV --> EC[Establish Control]
    INV --> PROD[Production]

    AP --> ST
    ST --> AG
```

This map follows the official rules reference entries for **Strategy Phase**, **Action Phase**, **Strategic Action**, **Movement**, **Space Cannon**, and **Invasion**. It is the basis for all phase tags below: **Strategy**, **Action**, **Activation**, **Movement**, **Space Cannon Offense**, **Space Combat**, **Invasion**, **Bombardment**, **Commit Ground Forces**, **Space Cannon Defense**, **Ground Combat**, **Production**, **Status**, **Agenda**, and a catch-all **Persistent / multi-phase** tag for cards that continuously modify one or more windows rather than triggering at a single printed step. citeturn20view0turn20view1turn20view4turn20view5turn21view0turn21view1turn21view3

## Card catalogues

### Strategy cards and officially replaced versions

The strategy-card pool is small enough to catalogue cleanly. The main version-sensitive cards are **Diplomacy**, **Construction**, and **Warfare**; the others remain functionally stable in the all-expansions catalogues. citeturn35view0turn35view1turn35view2turn35view3

The strategy-card catalogue also explicitly documents card and effect interactions that can interrupt or modify the Strategy Phase, including **Political Stability**, **Public Disgrace**, **Manipulate Investments**, and the base-vs-PoK difference on **Acquiescence**. That matters because some “phase-affecting cards” are not strategy cards themselves, but exist precisely to alter strategy-card timing. citeturn35view4

## Interaction notes and edge cases

The rules reference matters whenever card text crosses phases. A few examples illustrate the point. **Space Cannon** exists in **two different timing windows**—once after movement as **Space Cannon Offense**, and once during **Invasion** as **Space Cannon Defense**—so cards like **Graviton Laser System**, **Plasma Scoring**, **Maneuvering Jets**, **Experimental Battlestation**, **L4 Disruptors**, and **Dannel of the Tenth Ω** have to be tagged to the correct step. Treating them as one generic “space cannon” bucket loses rules accuracy. citeturn21view1turn21view0turn17view6turn17view7turn32view0turn28view0

Likewise, several cards bridge multiple phases. **Integrated Economy** converts **Establish Control** into an immediate **Production** window. **Acquiescence Ω** is not a Strategy-Phase swap effect anymore; it now triggers when the Winnu player **resolves a strategic action**. **Cybernetic Enhancements Ω** moved from **start of turn** to **Status Phase command-token gain**, and **Wormhole Generator Ω** moved from **start of status** to an **action/exhaust** timing. Those are exactly the kinds of changes that phase-mapped databases need to model explicitly instead of treating as cosmetic balance patches. citeturn30view1turn29view1turn32view0

A final caution concerns *Thunder’s Edge*. The official announcement and rulebook excerpts make clear that the expansion adds not only five factions and many new cards, but also **breakthroughs**, revised strategy cards, revised tech components, a legendary **Mecatol Rex**, and additional legendary planets. So if your end goal is a “single source of truth” rules engine or timing assistant, you should store **set identity**, **version tag**, **replacement chain**, and **phase tags** as separate fields rather than deriving timing only from card names. citeturn24search8turn24search0turn25search1turn25search3
