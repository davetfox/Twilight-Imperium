## Methodology

I prioritized sources in this order: **official Fantasy Flight rulebooks and rules references**, official **Fantasy Flight news/preview pages** that contained faction-sheet images or explicit component/version statements, then **secondary** community sources only where official text was not practically accessible. The official rules sources used for timing were the **PoK Living Rules Reference** and the official product/rule pages for **Twilight Imperium**, **Prophecy of Kings**, **Codex III**, and **ThunderŌĆÖs Edge**. For timing interpretation, I relied especially on the rules-reference treatment of faction-sheet abilities, component actions, persistent effects, and ŌĆ£when/after/startŌĆØ timing windows. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn22search7Ņłéturn14view7Ņłéturn15view0Ņłéturn1search5Ņłéturn12search0Ņłéturn1search2Ņłü

I treated **the most recent official print** as primary where variant text existed. In practice, that mattered for **Council Keleres**, where the accessible Omega index records ThunderŌĆÖs Edge revisions to **The Tribunii** and **LawŌĆÖs Order**; both the older and current texts are listed below. For the rest of the roster, I did not locate a confirmed faction-sheet text replacement in the accessible sources, so I marked them unchanged. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn19view0Ņłéturn17view0Ņłü

I also applied one necessary scope assumption: I **excluded faction-specific unit abilities printed elsewhere on the faction sheet**ŌĆöfor example flagship, mech, war sun, or space dock textŌĆöbecause the request asked for **faction-specific abilities that appear on faction player boards**, and the stronger, cleaner interpretation in TI4 rules language is the **faction ability panel**, not every non-card text box printed on the sheet. Where a single faction-sheet ability contains multiple timing clauses, I kept it as **one row** and listed **multiple phases/steps** in the same row.

If this had been done fully live from scratch, including source collection, image annotation, timing reconciliation, and CSV generation, the estimated completion time would have been **about 90ŌĆō120 minutes**.

## Decision Framework

The rules reference says faction-sheet abilities are abilities on a faction sheet, and some of them are perpetual effects. I used that, plus the trigger words on the printed ability text, to decide whether a line belonged in this report and where it sits in the turn structure. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn22search7Ņłéturn15view2Ņłü

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Text appears on a faction sheet] --> B{Is it in the faction-ability panel?}
    B -->|Yes| C[Include in this report]
    B -->|No| D{Is it unit, tech, promissory, leader, or other component text?}
    D -->|Yes| E[Exclude as not a faction-sheet ability]
    D -->|No| F[Review case-by-case]
    A -->|No| G[Exclude as card-based or external text]
```

A timing note that applies across the whole dataset: if the ability text says **ŌĆ£ActionŌĆØ**, it is a **component action** in the **Action Phase**; if it says **ŌĆ£whenŌĆØ**, it resolves at the event itself; if it says **ŌĆ£afterŌĆØ**, it resolves immediately after the event; if it has no trigger and instead changes a rule continuously, I treated it as a **persistent/perpetual effect**.
